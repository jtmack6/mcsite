title: Why I Switched to MacPorts
date: 2026-02-14T22:13:12.615Z
lastmod: 2026-02-14T22:54:56.499Z
---
Coming from a FreeBSD enthusiastic background, it somewhat made sense to build most of the development tools from scratch. I had used brew for years and was somewhat coerced into using asdf for some projects. After a while I felt brew was a bit too "out of the box" for development tools, yet perfect for managing applications like browsers and the larger toys available. asdf just got a bit too clunky when trying to managing sets of tools that needed to be configured in certain ways and was fed up the last time I had to delete and reshim everything to make one thing work.

While at a job, I decided to go against the grain and just use MacPorts for everything. After the initial install and getting around some work JAMF quirks I saw the benefits immediately. Being a fish shell dork I also whipped up some quick aliases to get myself around and after a while fixing my dev environment daily became a thing of the past.
